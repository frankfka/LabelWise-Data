Important Notice

On December 14, 2016, amendments to nutrition labelling, list of ingredients and food colour requirements of the Food and Drug Regulations came into force. Regulated parties have a five (5) year transition period to meet the new labelling requirements.

Consult the Former – Permitted Synonyms for Food Additives Table for information on the former requirements.

Common names listed in bold font in the table indicate that the particular name is listed in one of Health Canada's lists of permitted food additives. The common names listed in brackets refer to their corresponding French names.

This table only includes food additives with a permitted synonym or abbreviation, it does not list all food additives. Health Canada maintains and updates the lists of permitted food additives. The CFIA reviews and assesses synonyms for use as common names in the list of ingredients.
